Rationale
Why AI League Exists
The Problem
  • AI skill development is invisible — no one knows who's ahead or falling behind
  • Engineers have no incentive to optimise agent configs beyond "good enough"
  • Best practices stay locked in individual heads — never shared or stress-tested
  • AI transformation progress is slow when it's unglamorous and unmeasured
  • Token efficiency and cost consciousness aren't rewarded by delivery pressure alone
→
AI League
←
What It Delivers
  • Makes AI skill visible and measurable — scored, ranked, tracked over seasons
  • Creates a pull mechanism for capability uplift — engineers choose to get better
  • Forces best-practice sharing: community proposals reviewed and promoted to all
  • Rewards the behaviours the platform needs: quality, efficiency, cost-consciousness
  • Gives leadership a leading indicator for AI readiness — before it shows in delivery
Design principle

"If you can measure it and make it competitive, engineers will self-optimise. League turns a platform policy goal — efficient, safe, capable AI use — into something engineers actually want to win."

Scoring System
7-Dimension Scoring — How Winners Are Made

Scoring Weights

Quality — 35%
Output correctness against rubric. The primary signal — quality always wins over efficiency alone.
Robustness — 20%
Consistent performance across edge cases, adversarial prompts, and varied inputs.
Token Efficiency — 15%
Quality-per-token ratio. Rewards lean, precise prompting over verbose chains.
Speed — 10%
End-to-end latency of the agent run. Penalises unnecessary sequential steps.
Cost Efficiency — 10%
Monetary cost of completion. Rewards choosing cheaper models when quality holds.
Creativity — 5%
Novel approaches not seen in prior submissions. Encourages experimentation.
Improvement Rate — 5%
Score delta across your 3 attempts. Even if you don't top the leaderboard, consistent improvement earns points — League rewards learning, not just winning.
Composite score range: 0 – 1,000 per submission
