Explain the IFRS 17 discount rate methodology
IFRS 17 requires insurers to discount future cash flows using risk-free rates that reflect the currency and duration of insurance liabilities. The standard permits two approaches: the bottom-up method starts from a liquid, risk-free reference rate and adds an illiquidity premium; the top-down method begins with asset portfolio returns and strips out credit risk. The resulting discount rate curve must be derived consistently at each reporting date, reflecting current market conditions. Entities must also apply the variable fee approach for contracts with direct participation features, where the discount rate affects the contractual service margin unwinding pattern over the coverage period.

• Top-down approach: Asset portfolio yield minus credit spread — preferred when asset-liability matching is tight
• Reporting date refresh: Curves must be recalculated at every period end to reflect current market conditions
